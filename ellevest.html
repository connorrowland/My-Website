ENGINEERING

Ellevest

Ellevest is a digital investing platform geared towards women where I had my first job in engineering. My time there was awesome. The employees who work there are some of the most intelligent individuals I've had the opportunity to be around. I'll always be thankful for my time there, as I was able to learn so much and prepare myself for the next step in my engineering career.

WHEN I WORKED THERE

September 2015 – April 2017

WHAT I DID

My work at Ellevest began in the Fall of 2015 as a contractor where my first task was a simple build of their Beta release “Sign Up” page. Over the next year and a half, my work progressed into projects like out a promotional "Galentine's Day Page," the preliminary features of a CMS, Rails API endpoints, and all sorts of fun React components. I also spent a large amouint of time refining and adding to Ellevest's back-office application, gaining more strength in Ruby, Rails, and MYSQL.

Galentine's Day

Galentine's Day was a promotion Ellevest ran in honor of financial independence and Valentine's day. My job was to bring Galentine's day to life in React components, and allow users to send promotional cards to other people through the page. If you look below, you'll find an example of how this worked on screen.

In-Office Tools

A huge part of this work dealt with visually cleaning up the front end of these in-office tools by making them responsive and more appealing to use. Some pages had text and data pieces spreading out of view, which meant that potentially important things just couldn't be seen on a page, which was bad. So I cleaned up some of these tools with some fresh CSS, making them more functional and easy to use. Additionally, some of the features needed to be refactored RESTfully. People in the office wanted to be able to simply modify a URL to retrieve a resource, and those URLs needed more consistency throughout the application to enable people to do that.

Press Page

Another project I had was building out a simple and easy to use page to hold all of Ellevest's press articles. I built the Press Page in a similar manner to that of the Resource Center, so it could easily integrate into the CMS as it became more developed. That way, Ellevest employees could add to it easily as Ellevest was featured in the press more.
